The dimensions of the sample area are always a box and cannot be changed!

The sample size is the amount of little squares that are sampled from the image MUST BE WITHIN ASPECT RATIO OF IMAGE. !! BIG IMPACT ON PERFORMANCE/TIME TILL OUTPUT !!

For example, if the image is a 9:9 aspect ratio, you could use 60 or 30 or 150. The image has to be big enough for some of those though.


The threshold is the amount of leeway the algorithm gives the image when comparing. 3 is a good starting number, but feel free to experiment all you want.

The bigger the number, the more accurate the algorithm will probably be, but it will also probably miss more spots!!!!!


The pixel Perfect checks box makes the sample size the exact width and height of the image, so the boxes are exactly one pixel on the image.

This will give the best, most clear results, but will take the longest to process, especially if the image is a high-quality image.
